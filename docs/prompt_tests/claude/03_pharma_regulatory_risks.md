# Regulatory Risks Facing Major Pharmaceutical Companies

## Key Insight
All major pharma companies face a common set of intensifying regulatory risks: drug pricing controls (especially the IRA), FDA/global approval complexity, data privacy, manufacturing compliance, and anti-corruption requirements. Responses focus on compliance investment, formulary access, and legal/legislative engagement.

---

## Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)

**Key Risks:**
- "The general trend is toward increasingly stringent regulation and enforcement" across all markets [JNJ, 10-K, 2023-02-16, Item 8]
- FDA can ban products, seize goods, impose criminal penalties, or recommend DOJ prosecution
- **Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)**: Signed August 2022, authorizes Medicare price-setting for high-spend drugs starting 2026 (Part D) and 2028 (Part B); JNJ products could be selected
- IRA also imposes inflation-linked rebates beginning October 2022 (Part D) and January 2023 (Part B)
- Expanding data privacy laws (5 U.S. states + China's PIPL in 2022); EU enforcement fines increasing
- Supply chain complexity subject to growing regulatory requirements

**Response:**
- Monitoring IRA implementation decisions
- Investing in compliance with cGMP and privacy regulations
- Engaging with evolving transparency/disclosure requirements for healthcare provider payments [JNJ, 10-K, 2023-02-16, Item 8]

---

## Pfizer (PFE)

**Key Risks:**
- Subject to "extensive regulation by government authorities" covering approvals, manufacturing, pricing, data privacy, and anti-corruption [PFE, 10-K, 2025-02-27, Risk Factors]
- **IRA pricing**: Eliquis was among the first 10 drugs selected for Medicare Drug Price Negotiation Program (August 2023) [PFE, 10-K, 2025-02-27, Business]
- MCO formulary exclusions increasingly placing branded drugs on higher tiers or non-preferred status
- **340B Program**: Pfizer implemented a contract pharmacy integrity policy; facing state-level legal challenges
- **Data privacy**: Rapidly evolving, conflicting jurisdictional requirements; increasing enforcement and litigation
- **EU reform**: EC proposed comprehensive overhaul of EU medicines regulation (April 2023); could reduce regulatory data protection from 8 to 6 years
- **China VBP**: Pfizer "mostly not been successful in the VBP bidding process" for off-patent products [PFE, 10-K, 2024-02-22, Risk Factors]
- Nitrosamine contamination risk (recalled all Chantix lots in 2021; working toward reintroduction)

**Response:**
- Developing "stronger support designed to demonstrate the net value of medicines and vaccines" [PFE, 10-K, 2024-02-22, Risk Factors]
- Active supplier management and advanced purchasing to mitigate supply risks
- Investing in anti-counterfeit technologies and law enforcement partnerships
- Engaging regulators on nitrosamine guidance and resubmissions [PFE, 10-K, 2025-02-27, Business]

---

## Merck (MRK)

**Key Risks:**
- **IRA direct impact**: Januvia selected for 2026 price-setting; Janumet/Janumet XR selected for 2027; **Keytruda expected to be selected for 2028** — "U.S. sales of Keytruda will decline after that time" [MRK, 10-K, 2025-02-25, Business]
- American Rescue Plan eliminated Medicaid rebate cap (January 2024), potentially requiring rebates exceeding 100% of AMP
- Potential FDA/CDC policy changes under new U.S. administration could delay approvals and reduce vaccine recommendations
- Japan mandates annual price reductions; China's NRDL negotiations averaged **63% price reductions** for new entries [MRK, 10-K, 2025-02-25, Financial Statements]
- Emerging market risks: political instability, compulsory licensing threats, currency controls

**Response:**
- Monitoring policy changes; committed to access-to-medicines principles
- Pursuing "breakthrough therapy" designation to accelerate FDA review
- Systematic pipeline evaluation for low/middle-income country potential [MRK, 10-K, 2025-02-25, Business]

---

## Eli Lilly (LLY)

**Key Risks:**
- FDA holds "broad discretion" to interpret approval conditions; inadequate agency staffing poses risk of delays [LLY, 10-K, 2026-02-12, Business]
- Post-approval risks: FDA can mandate labeling changes, post-marketing studies, or withdraw approval
- Patent challenges via IPR proceedings using lower standard of proof than federal court
- U.S. government proposals for "march-in rights" could undermine patent protections
- Increasing generic/biosimilar patent challenges internationally

**Response:**
- "Substantial investments of capital and operating expenses" in company-wide quality systems for cGMP compliance
- Extensive post-marketing surveillance programs
- Engaging in patent litigation and USPTO proceedings to defend IP [LLY, 10-K, 2026-02-12, Business]

---

## AbbVie (ABBV)

**Key Risks:**
- Regulatory approvals costly and time-consuming; no guarantee of approval for new indications
- Post-approval safety/efficacy issues can trigger product withdrawal or label narrowing
- Single-source supplier dependencies create regulatory compliance exposure
- Anti-kickback, false claims, and FCPA compliance requirements
- International pricing controls: "many countries control the price of health care products directly or indirectly" [ABBV, 10-K, 2025-02-14, Business]

**Response:**
- Significant capital investment in cGMP compliance and quality systems
- Self-insures product liability (determined insurance costs outweigh benefits)
- Compliance programs for anti-corruption (FCPA, UK Bribery Act) and healthcare fraud laws [ABBV, 10-K, 2025-02-14, Business]

## Common Themes Across All Companies

| Risk Category | Universal Challenge |
|---|---|
